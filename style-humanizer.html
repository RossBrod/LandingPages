Same motion. Same law. One sounds like a template. The other sounds like you.

Robotic AI Draft

Plaintiff respectfully moves this Court to compel Defendant to provide complete responses to discovery. Defendant's current responses are inadequate and fail to comply with the Federal Rules of Civil Procedure.

First, Defendant's objection to Interrogatory No. 5 lacks merit. The information requested is relevant to the claims and defenses in this action. Second, Defendant's response to Request for Production No. 12 is incomplete because it does not include all responsive documents.

For the foregoing reasons, Plaintiff requests that the Court grant this motion and order Defendant to provide complete responses within fourteen days.

Humanized Draft

The record is clear: Defendant's discovery responses are not merely deficient, they are obstructionist. Four months into litigation, Plaintiff still lacks basic documents central to its claims.

Consider Interrogatory No. 5. Defendant claims "relevance" shields internal communications about the product defect. Indeed, these are the very communications that prove knowledge. See Fed. R. Civ. P. 26(b)(1) (scope extends to any matter relevant to any party's claim or defense).

The Court should compel complete responses within ten days. Critically, further delay prejudices Plaintiff's ability to meet the discovery cutoff. Half-measures will not suffice.
