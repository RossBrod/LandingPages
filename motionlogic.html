What It Does

It Begins with Constraints, Not Arguments

MotionLogic does not start with a motion name. It does not assume a legal theory. It starts from the litigation problem and works backward through procedural logic to determine what the court is permitted to do.

  • Classifies the procedural issue — clerical, jurisdictional, merits-based, or interlocutory
  • Determines court authority — what the court is legally allowed to consider and decide
  • Identifies motions that must not be filed — those that waive rights, expand exposure, or invite reconsideration
  • Enforces standards and scope — before a single argument is written
  • Builds a constrained motion architecture — required sections only, correct sequencing, forbidden arguments excluded

Core Capabilities

Five Layers of Procedural Control

Each layer enforces a constraint. Together they prevent silent procedural failure from reaching the court.

01 Priority

Procedural Issue Classification

If you misclassify the issue, everything that follows fails. The classifier converts the litigation problem into a procedural category that governs all downstream logic.

02

Motion Selection Engine

Not every motion that can be written should be filed. The engine selects the correct procedural vehicle, ranks alternatives, and flags motions that are dangerous or waived.

  • Safe CCP §473(b) — Correction of clerical error in minute order
  • Caution CCP §1008 — Reconsideration (new facts required; scope risk)
  • Reject CCP §473(d) — Void order (overbroad; invites modification)
Decision-as-a-Service: the system decides which procedural vehicles are safe to use.
03 Priority

Standards & Scope Generator

Courts decide cases by rules, not arguments. This generator produces the governing standard for any selected motion—authority, scope, decision rule, and relief type.

Standards & Scope — CCP §473(b)

Authority
Inherent power to correct clerical errors in orders
Scope
Limited to ministerial mistakes; no substantive reconsideration
Decision Rule
Mandatory correction if error is clerical and record supports it
Relief
Mandatory (not discretionary)
04 Priority

Constrained Motion Scaffolding

Structure before persuasion. The scaffolding builder produces a motion architecture with required sections only, in correct order, with forbidden arguments explicitly excluded.

  1. I. Notice of Motion Required
  2. II. Statement of Facts (record-only) Required
  3. III. Identification of Clerical Error Required
  4. IV. Governing Standard: §473(b) Locked
  5. V. Proposed Corrected Order Required
  6. VI. Argument re: merits of underlying ruling Excluded
  7. VII. Request for modification of support Excluded
05
